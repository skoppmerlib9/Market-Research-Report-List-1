<p><p>The pepper market primarily consists of black and white pepper, both derived from the same plant but processed differently. Black pepper is harvested when the berries are still green and cooked briefly before drying, resulting in a strong flavor and aroma. In contrast, white pepper is made from fully mature berries, soaked to remove the outer layer, leading to a milder taste. Both types are essential in global cuisine, influencing market dynamics based on demand, quality, and regional preferences.</p></p>
