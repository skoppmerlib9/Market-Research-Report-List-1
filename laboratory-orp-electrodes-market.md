<p><ul><li>Single Junction</li><li>Double Junction</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Laboratory ORP (Oxidation-Reduction Potential) electrodes are crucial for measuring the electron activity in solutions. They come in two main types: single junction and double junction. Single junction electrodes have a single reference solution, making them simpler but potentially more susceptible to contamination. In contrast, double junction electrodes feature two separate junctions, providing enhanced protection against sample interference and contamination. This design improves accuracy and longevity, making double junction electrodes preferable for complex or challenging sample environments.</p></p>
