<p><p>The Japanese Encephalitis Virus Vaccine market includes two primary types: inactivated vaccines and live attenuated vaccines. Inactivated vaccines consist of virus particles that have been killed, providing immunity without the risk of causing disease, making them suitable for high-risk populations. In contrast, live attenuated vaccines use weakened forms of the virus, offering a robust immune response but with a potential risk of mild disease in immunocompromised individuals. Both types play crucial roles in preventing Japanese encephalitis in endemic regions.</p></p>
