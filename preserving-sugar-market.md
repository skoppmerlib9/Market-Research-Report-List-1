<p><p>The preserving sugar market encompasses three main types: powder, granules, and syrup. Powdered sugar, finely ground for easy dissolving, is ideal for baking and confectionery. Granulated sugar, in larger crystals, is versatile for various preserving methods like canning and fermenting. Syrups, which combine sugar and water, serve as a concentrated preserving agent, often used for fruits and jams. Each type caters to specific culinary needs, ensuring efficient preservation while enhancing flavor and texture in food products.</p></p>
