<p><p>Thyroid Eye Disease (TED) treatments encompass various approaches, including medication, radioactive iodine therapy, and surgery. Medication primarily involves corticosteroids to reduce inflammation and immunosuppressants to manage symptoms. Radioactive iodine therapy aims to correct hyperthyroidism by targeting thyroid cells, thereby indirectly benefiting eye symptoms. Surgery may be necessary for severe cases, focusing on decompression to relieve pressure on the optic nerve or correcting eyelid retraction. Each treatment type addresses specific aspects of TED, improving patient outcomes and quality of life.</p></p>
