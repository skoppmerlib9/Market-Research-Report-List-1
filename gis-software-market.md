<p><p>The GIS software market is primarily divided into two types: on-premise and cloud-based solutions. On-premise GIS software is installed locally on user hardware, offering greater control and data security, but requiring substantial maintenance and infrastructure investment. In contrast, cloud-based GIS solutions deliver flexibility and scalability, allowing users to access data and tools remotely through the internet. This model reduces upfront costs, facilitates collaboration, and ensures automatic updates, making it increasingly popular among organizations looking for efficient geographic data management.</p></p>
