<p><p>The meal fibers market comprises two main types: soluble and insoluble fibers. Soluble fibers dissolve in water, forming a gel-like substance that aids in digestion, helps regulate blood sugar levels, and lowers cholesterol. They are commonly found in oats, fruits, and legumes. Insoluble fibers do not dissolve in water, promoting bowel regularity and preventing constipation. They are prevalent in whole grains, nuts, and vegetables. Both types are essential for a balanced diet, driving demand in health-focused food products.</p></p>
