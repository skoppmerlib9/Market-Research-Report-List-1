<p><p>The Aircraft Carbon Braking System market consists of two primary types: pneumatic and hydraulic systems. Pneumatic braking systems use compressed air to engage the brakes, providing quick responsiveness and efficiency, making them suitable for lighter aircraft. In contrast, hydraulic braking systems utilize fluid pressure to operate the brakes, offering greater force and consistent performance, particularly in larger, heavier aircraft. Both types leverage advanced carbon materials for improved heat resistance and performance, contributing to enhanced safety and reliability in aviation.</p></p>
